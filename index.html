SMART Process in Dashboard Development

S — Specific
Defined Purpose

Dashboard scope is clearly defined: monitor PTA/PX performance, EXIM trade flows, and daily pricing movements — not generic reporting

S
M — Measurable
Clear KPIs

Every dashboard has quantifiable metrics: Value (USD), Quantity (KG), MoM growth %, shipment count, price delta — all tracked over time

M
A — Achievable
Data Ready

A
R — Relevant
Business-Driven

Directly supports marketing, sales, and operations decisions: pricing strategy, supply planning, and trade optimization

R
T — Time-Bound
Auto-Refresh

Dashboards configured with scheduled daily/monthly refresh — ensuring data is always current without manual updates

T
Key Insight: Applying SMART ensured dashboards were built with clear business intent — not just aesthetically appealing visuals, but truly decision-enabling tools.

Section 07C · Daily Pricing — Fully Automated Pipeline

Daily Pricing Monitor — Automation Deep Dive

📋 Input — Raw Excel Data (e.g. "Mar Average" Sheet)
📊 Output — Automated Power BI Dashboard
⚙️

Automation Logic — Key Business Concepts

① Automatic Sheet Detection
Text.Contains([Name], "Average")
What it means Automatically detects and ingests new monthly sheets — e.g., "Mar Average" — without any manual configuration. Just add a new sheet and Power Query picks it up.
② Dynamic Month Extraction
Text.BeforeDelimiter([Name], " ")
What it means Automatically extracts the month name from the sheet tab (e.g., "Mar" from "Mar Average"). No hardcoding required — works for every month automatically.
③ Transformation Pipeline
fn_TransformDaily([SheetData], [MonthName], [MonthNumber])
What it means A reusable function converts messy, multi-column Excel data into a clean, structured daily dataset ready for Power BI — applied to every sheet automatically.
④ Data Cleaning
Table.SelectRows(...)
What it means Removes empty rows, invalid entries, and zero-value records — ensuring only quality data reaches the dashboard. No manual cleaning required.
⑤ Daily Change Calculation
([Price] - [PrevPrice]) / [PrevPrice]
What it means Calculates day-over-day price movement as a percentage. Instantly surfaces market volatility with color-coded △ indicators in the dashboard.
⑥ Product Classification
Text.Contains([Product], ...)
What it means Automatically categorizes raw column names into business products:
PX PTA Naphtha Benzene Ethylene Spread Crude
⑦ Monthly Average
List.Average(...)
What it means Automatically calculates monthly average price per product. Always up-to-date — no manual spreadsheet averaging needed by the team.
⑧ Product Mapping
Products = { Column → Product → Category → Unit }
What it means Maps raw Excel column headers to structured business metadata: product name, category, and unit — turning unstructured data into a clean, reportable dataset.

Section 10

Challenge & Response

⚠️ Challenges Faced

📋
Inconsistent Data Formats

Different teams used different date formats, column naming conventions, and unit representations — making consolidation impossible without manual fixing.

🕐
Manual Reporting Inefficiency

Weekly reports required 2–4 hours of manual compilation — prone to errors and always one step behind current business reality.

🔁
Data Duplication & Silos

Multiple versions of the same data existed across departments, causing confusion and contradicting reports — leading to distrust in numbers.

✅ How We Responded

📐
Standardized Excel Input Templates

Designed locked input templates with data validation rules, standardized column headers, and enforced date/unit formats — eliminating format inconsistency at the source.

⚡
Automated Power BI Dashboards

Replaced manual Excel reports with automated dashboards that refresh daily — reducing reporting effort from hours to zero while improving accuracy and timeliness.

🔧
Power Query Data Cleaning Logic

Built reusable Power Query scripts to detect and flag duplicates, normalize formats, and merge data from all sources into a single verified master dataset.
